# it's been a while

it's been about . . . *checks calendar* five months now. i still own a vps because i bought a year of it.
will i renew it? i don't know.

i'm kind of excited for self-hosting my future endeavors, and i know i'm the type to have a server rack in the bedroom.
that said, there is comfort and privacy that comes with using a vps, and if i want to host more public-facing sites and repos, maybe i'll keep the vps.

or maybe i won't.
i've got time.

## ok, but . . . it's been five months!
**yeah**

so what happened? well, as much as i love little projects i think webdev and the idea of my own website was more or less a phase?
i don't really care to build websites . . . at least not crazy ones. i think the tech hype of a new framework every week drives me away.
if i were to try again, i'd probably just do plain html, css, and javascript. there's joy in building something from nothing, and i think frameworks take away from that.

the main change is at my real job, y'know, the paying kind. there's new opportunity there, but it requires me to study, train skillsets, and focus.
the downside? i haven't played with any projects really. and that upsets me. i love to code. i love coding so much. i haven't stopped thinking about coding for months now.
yet i don't really code often. why code? i have work to focus on. i have to focus on my job. i have to study for my upcoming (hopefully) responsibilities.

sadly, i have this adhd blockage in my life:
if i need to do "thing" but i want to do "other thing" or "this thing", guess what i do?
nothing.

doing something that isn't the "thing" i need to do, is bad. it's bad to do something when i should do "thing"
but if i don't want to do "thing" or feel up to "thing", i just do nothing.

## how to fix this
well, i think the easiest way is to fight myself as much as i can
stop thinking about what to do, why to do it, or how to do it.
turn the brain off and just do *something*

this post, is me doing *something*.
fifteen minute break at work, i wrote a post on my website i haven't touched in 5 months.
that's *something*

and i'm happy about this.

**next time i'll come back with some projects or things i've coded. for now, back to work.**
